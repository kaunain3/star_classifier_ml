What kind of star is it?

Enter a star's physical measurements and the model will classify it into one of 6 stellar types.

Physical measurements

Surface temperature in Kelvin. Most stars range from 2,000K (cool red) to 40,000K (hot blue).

Brightness relative to the Sun (L/Lo). Our Sun = 1. Hypergiants can reach 800,000.

Size relative to the Sun (R/Ro). Our Sun = 1. Brown dwarfs are less than 0.1.

Intrinsic brightness. Lower (even negative) means brighter. Sun is about +4.8.

Classification

Observed color of the star, which relates to its surface temperature.

Harvard spectral classification. O is hottest, M is coolest.
